In the early 2000's Peck collaborated on several pieces with David Dorfman Dance, toured extensively with John Jasperse Company performing his live electronic score for justtwodancers, and served as a faculty member at the American Dance Festival. Since then Peck's dance collaborations have included pieces with Eleanor Bauer, Milka Djordjevich, Dance Forum Taipei, and RoseAnne Spradlin. The Brooklyn Adult Recorder choir, which Peck organized with in 2005 with choreographer Beth Gill, is notorious in some circles for its minimalist durational performances. Peck performs as an improviser with Listening Music for the Age of Crystal Moon Cone along with Jon Moniaci and Stephen Rush. The trio has been joined in concert by legendary composer/accordionist Pauline Oliveros, and recently recorded in a room adjacent to the room housing George Winston's piano. Peck has received numerous grants to support his work from institutions such as the American Music Center and the MAP Fund. He is currently on a multi-year fellowship at the University of Virginia, where he is pursuing a PhD in Composition and Computer Technologies. He is also a fellow in the Praxis program at U.Va.'s Scholars' Lab, a major center for research in digital humanities.
